My Research Areas

My research focuses on the mathematical analysis of complex systems described by partial differential equations, especially in the context of control, geometry, and multiscale phenomena. Key areas include:

  • Asymptotic analysis of PDEs and optimal control problems: We analyze the limiting behavior of solutions to PDEs posed in irregular or rapidly oscillating domains. This helps in understanding stability, convergence, and effective behavior of the considered problem.
  • Simultaneous homogenization and dimension reduction: This dual technique is crucial for studying physical phenomena such as electromagnetic shielding, particularly in thin structures. I work on rigorous models to analyze such problems where fine-scale geometry and material properties interact.
  • Homogenization in complex geometric structures: We develop mathematical frameworks and operator-theoretic tools for homogenizing PDEs in domains that lack classical periodicity, such as branched, curved, or quasi-periodic microstructures. These advances have implications for engineering, material science, and physics.
  • Lately, I have developed an interest in parabolic partial differential equations, particularly focusing on issues related to their controllability. Fluid-Structure Interaction: This area involves analyzing systems where a solid body interacts with a surrounding fluid. The fluid’s nature—whether inviscid or viscous, compressible or incompressible—dictates the governing equations, leading to different mathematical models. These interactions are relevant across numerous disciplines, including physics, engineering, and biology
